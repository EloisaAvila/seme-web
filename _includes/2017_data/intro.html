About

87% of children in Sub-Saharan Africa fail to achieve reading profiency in their respective languages. In developing contexts like the Côte d'Ivoire, support for education systems is very important for a variety of reasons. Through the support of organizations such as Pratham and the introduction of programs like Teaching at the Right Level, communities are able to increase the success of their students.

Students in rural areas of the cacao farming regions of the Côte d'Ivoire often find themselves held home from school to help their parents harvest. This creates instability in the child's education, as they progress in age, but often miss so much schooling that they are unable to catch up and become frustrated, creating even more of a barrier for them.

Teaching at the Right Level is a program that reorganizes students into "levels" based on their tested knowledge, rather than their age. This allows students to be more engaged in their education and ensures that they are able to progress at their own speed. A non-governmental organization called Pratham implemented this teaching program in multiple developing contexts, where it achieved success, before coming to the Côte d'Ivoire. Now they are working to train the teachers in the new curriculum and provide long-term support to them through their government ministry.

Currently, teachers are mentored through visits by ministry officials, but infrastructural challenges (i.e. poor roads, travel cost, etc) and lack of mentors reduce the frequency of visits. Our initial research suggests that teachers receive mentoring visits once or twice in an academic year. This, coupled with lack of access to resources, makes it hard for teachers to successfully implement Teaching at the Right Level. The SEME Team is working to identify and provide a support system using technology that the teachers already live with to provide them with a medium to ask questions and receive support consistently rather than only when supervisors are able to physically visit the schools.
